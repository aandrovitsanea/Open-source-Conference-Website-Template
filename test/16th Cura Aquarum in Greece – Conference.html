Welcome to the 16th conference Cura Aquarum in Greece

January 30, 2015March 24, 2015Leave a comment

It is our great pleasure that it has been possible for the German Water History Association (DWhG) to organise the 16th conference Cura Aquarum this year in Athens, which is a conference on the topic of ancient hydraulic systems.

It is more or less impossible to plan a conference in a foreign country without a local partner. For this reason, it was a special pleasure that the German Archaeological Institute in Athens (DAI Athens) helped us with words and deeds. We are grateful to Ms. Prof. Dr. Katja Sporn and Mr. Dr. Senff for this support.

We would therefore like to extend a heartfelt thank you to the Ephorates of Athens, East Attica, West Attica & Piraeus and Corinth and representatives of the various excavation sites for their understanding and support, most notably Ms. Dr.Mpanou, Ms. Dr. Lazaridou, Ms. Dr. Andrikou, Mr. Dr.Kissas, Ms. Dr. Chrisoulaki.

The goal of the conference, including the lectures and the excursions, is to bring scientists from various disciplines together for a fruitful exchange of ideas.

We wish you all an interesting, fruitful and harmonious conference Cura Aquarum in Athens.

Anna Androvitsanea

Henning Fahlbusch
